## 1. Executive Summary

As `OpenHTTPA` matures into a production-ready protocol for high-assurance communication, the strategic focus shifts from "Secure Transport" to "Universal Trust Fabric." This roadmap outlines five major feature vectors designed to capture the **Verified AI**, **Enterprise Governance**, and **Scalable Attestation** markets.

---

## 2. Strategic Feature Vectors

### 2.1 Verified AI (V-AI) & Trusted Inference

- **Goal**: Provide hardware-backed provenance for AI model outputs.
- **Mechanism**: Integrate TEE-bound LLMs (via `openhttpa-llm`) with ZK-based proofs of execution. The client receives a signed `OpenHTTPA` response containing the LLM output _and_ a cryptographic proof that the output was generated by a specific model (hash) on a specific input (hash).
- **Strategic Impact**: Establishes `OpenHTTPA` as the standard for "Agentic Trust" in the AI economy.

### 2.2 ZK-Aggregated Attestation (ZAA)

- **Goal**: Solve the "DCAP Bloat" problem for resource-constrained clients.
- **Mechanism**: Use the `openhttpa-zk` crate to wrap complex hardware quotes (SGX DCAP, TDX) into a concise ZK-SNARK.
- **Strategic Impact**: Enables `OpenHTTPA` adoption on mobile, IoT, and embedded devices that cannot perform heavy X.509/DCAP chain verification.

### 2.3 Dynamic Policy-as-Code (PaC)

- **Goal**: Move from static configuration to dynamic, fine-grained authorization.
- **Mechanism**: Integrate **Open Policy Agent (OPA)** into the `openhttpa-mesh` and `openhttpa-server`. Policies defined in Rego can evaluate attestation data, geographic location, and metadata to make real-time admission decisions.
- **Strategic Impact**: Critical for Enterprise compliance and multi-tenant TEE environments.

### 2.4 Multi-Vendor TEE Federation

- **Goal**: Eliminate single-vendor dependency and hardware lock-in.
- **Mechanism**: Standardize the `openhttpa-tee` abstraction to allow seamless trust established between different TEE types (e.g., an Intel TDX node verifying an AMD SEV-SNP node).
- **Strategic Impact**: Essential for cloud-agnostic "Confidential Computing" deployments.

### 2.5 Post-Quantum Attestation (PQA)

- **Goal**: Future-proof the protocol's root-of-trust.
- **Mechanism**: Transition the `OpenHTTPA` attestation layer (AHL Binders and Handshake Signatures) to NIST-standardized PQC (ML-DSA).
- **Strategic Impact**: Positions `OpenHTTPA` as the most secure protocol in the post-quantum era.
